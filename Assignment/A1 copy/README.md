We ask whether the belief of relationship growth affects the intergroup segregation.
Schelling model demonstrates that, when agents have a certain degree of preference for similar neighbors, segregation will emerge. 
However, this model ignores the fact that relationships between agents are not static, and can be improved over time. Contageon model will be a more realistic alternative, which includes the concept of relationship growth: if two agents have been neighbors for a long time, they will have a higher chance of being happy with each other.
Despite that, there is still a missing piece in the contageon model: agents are not aware of the growth rate of their relationships. As Growth mindset theory suggests, if two agents believe that their relationship will improve over time, they will be more likely to invest in it, thus more likely to be happy with each other, which might mitigate the segregation.
Specifically, we consider the happiness to be malleable: if two agents have been neighbors for a long time, they will have a higher chance of being happy with each other. Furthermore, we introduce the relational growth mindset: whether an agent believes that her relationship will improve over time. We compare three models-classic Schelling model, contageon model, and growth mindset model——in terms of intergroup segregation.
Our hypothesis is that relational growth mindset will mitigate the segregation, whereas simple contact will not. This provides a potential intervention to mitigate the segregation.
